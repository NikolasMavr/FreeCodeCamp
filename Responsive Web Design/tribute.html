Carl Friedrich Gauss

"The greatest mathematician since antiquity"

Timeline of Carl Friedrich Gauss:

  • 1777 - Born in Brunswick, Principality of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel
  • 1791 - Introduced to the Duke of Brunswick, who awards him an annual scholarship. Gauss would receive financial support from the Duke until 1806
  • 1795-1798 - Studies at the recently established U. of Göttingen. In the fall of 1798, he leaves without a diploma and returns to Brunswick
  • 1799 - Submits (at the Duke’s request) a doctoral dissertation to the U. of Helmstedt, containing a proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
  • 1801 - Publication of Disquisitiones Artihmeticae, the foundational work of algebraic number theory
  • 1805 - Marriage to Johanna Osthoff. She would die after giving birth to their second child in 1809. On the same year, Gauss marries Minna Waldeck, daughter of a law professor at Göttingen
  • 1807 - Accepts position at Göttingen, where he would be in charge of the new observatory
  • 1812-1818 - Work on elliptic integrals, the arithmetic-geometric mean, and hyper geometric functions most of it unpublished, or published posthumously
  • 1818-1832 - Personally directs the geodetic survey of the Kingdom of Hanover. This leads to two publications in theoretical geodesy
  • 1831 - Death of Gauss’s second wife Minna. Gauss had two surviving children from his first marriage and three from his second
  • 1832 - Investigations on terrestrial magnetism, encouraged by Weber and Alexander v. Humboldt. The goal was to map out the magnetic field of the earth, including local and temporal variations
  • 1838 - First functioning electromagnetic telegraph, developed by Gauss and Weber
  • 1855 - Gauss dies. His brain has been incorporated in the anatomical collection of the University of Göttingen
